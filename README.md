
# Coffee Shop Sales Analysis ☕️

An end-to-end data analysis project focused on understanding consumer behavior, peak sales periods, and product performance for a retail coffee shop.

## 🎨 Design Concept
To match the "Coffee Shop" vibe, I utilized a warm, earthy color palette (Mocha, Cream, and Espresso tones). The goal was to create a dashboard that feels as professional as a high-end café’s internal report.

## 🛠️ Project Workflow

1. **Data Cleaning (Power Query):**
   - Standardized time formats and categorized transaction hours into "Morning", "Afternoon", and "Night".
   - Cleaned product categories to ensure accurate revenue tracking.

2. **Exploratory Data Analysis (SQL):**
   - Wrote queries to calculate the **Average Order Value (AOV)** to see how much customers spend per visit.
   - Identified the most popular coffee items by transaction volume versus total revenue.
   - Validated monthly sales trends to confirm seasonal spikes.

3. **Data Visualization (Power BI):**
   - **Overview Dashboard:** Tracking $112.25K in revenue with a $31.65 Average Order Value.
   - **Orders Study:** Breaking down which items sell fastest and at what time of day.
   - **Revenue Breakdown:** Identifying which products are the biggest "money makers."

## 🚀 Key Insights from the Data

* **Peak Seasons:** Sales aren't flat—there are clear spikes in **March ($15.9K)** and **October ($13.9K)**, suggesting seasonal promotions or weather-related demand.
* **The "Latte" Leader:** While "Americano with Milk" has the highest order volume (809 orders), **Lattes** are the top revenue generator, contributing significantly more to the bottom line.
* **Time of Day:** Sales are remarkably consistent across **Morning, Afternoon, and Night**, with the "Afternoon" shift leading slightly in total order count (1205 orders).
* **Efficiency:** The Average Order Value (AOV) of **$31.65** suggests that customers are often buying more than just a single cup of coffee per transaction.

## 📂 Repository Contents
* `Coffee_Shop_Queries.sql`: SQL scripts used for data deep-dives.
* `Coffee_Shop_Dashboard.pbix`: The full interactive Power BI file.
* `Coffee_Shop_Data.csv`: The raw transaction dataset.
